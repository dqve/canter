DRILLING RIG KITS - DIY RIG BUILDS

Drill Rig Kits allow customers to assemble their own drill rig.

Rig Kits allow customers to assemble their own drilling rig. This cost effective option to an assembled drill unit includes complete and painted fabrication, hydraulic components, diesel engine, tracks, drill head, everything but nuts and bolts and hydraulic hoses. These items are inexpensive and easily sourced locally.

Rig Kits can also be purchased in part, i.e. wihout tracks allowing you to fit the unit to a truck bed, as one example. Kits also allow you to incorporate non-standard accessories or customizations to suit your application/operation.

RIG KITS FAQ's

Q - What is a Rig Kit?
A - A Rig Kit is a partially built drilling rig. Final assembly is done by the customer lowering the purchase cost.
Q - What is included in the Rig Kit?
A - All fabrication, major hydraulic components, an engine, crawler tracks etc. Everything except nuts and bolts and hydraulic hoses.
Q - Why is a hydraulic hose kit not included?
A - Each hose would have to be run exactly as intended and in the correct order as their lengths would be fixed. A hose kit could contain 40+ hoses. This significantly lengthens the build time and overall cost of the kit. Hoses are quick to make up and easy to assemble on site during the build.
Q - What Rigs are available as kits?
A - All of them. However for the larger rigs, like the K60, an over head gantry/crane is recommended for safe assembly.
Q - Can anyone build a kit? Do you provide instructions?
A - Yes and Yes. We provide a detailed assembly manual and unlimited technical support during your build.
Q - What is included in the assembly manual?
A - Instructions. Assembly diagrams of each sub-assembly showing all parts and their positions. A bill of materials. Hydraulic circuit diagrams. Saftey notes. Drilling proceedures and more.
Q - What do the hydraulic circuit diagrams show?
A -The circuit diagrams in the manual show all the hydraulic components with easy to follow hose lines of how they connect. They also show hose sizes and direction of flow.
Q - How much do the kits cost?
A - Depending on the rig model and options , as much as $30k less than the assembled unit.
